# Self Exercise - 1.1

## Q1. What is data analysis, and why is it important in decision-making?
**Answer:**  
Data analysis is the process of collecting, cleaning, transforming, and interpreting data to extract meaningful insights.  
It is important because it helps make informed, evidence-based decisions.

---

## Q2. Explain the different types of data analysis (descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, prescriptive).
**Answer:**  
- Descriptive: What happened?  
- Diagnostic: Why did it happen?  
- Predictive: What will happen?  
- Prescriptive: What should be done?

---

## Q3. Mention two real-world examples where data analysis is applied.
**Answer:**  
- E-commerce: Product recommendations  
- Healthcare: Disease prediction and diagnosis  

---

## Q4. What are the key steps in planning a data science project?
**Answer:**  
- Define problem  
- Collect data  
- Clean and preprocess data  
- Explore data  
- Build model  
- Evaluate model  
- Deploy solution  

---

## Q5. Why is defining the problem statement crucial before starting a project?
**Answer:**  
It ensures clarity of goals, proper data selection, and efficient use of resources.

---

## Q6. What role does data collection and preprocessing play in a data science project?
**Answer:**  
It ensures data quality, removes noise, handles missing values, and prepares data for modeling.

---

## Q7. What does it mean to frame a machine learning problem?
**Answer:**  
It means defining the type of problem (classification, regression, clustering) and selecting inputs and outputs properly.

---

## Q8. How do you decide whether to use a supervised, unsupervised, or reinforcement learning approach?
**Answer:**  
- Supervised: Labeled data available  
- Unsupervised: No labels  
- Reinforcement: Learning via rewards and actions  

---

## Q9. Why is identifying the target variable important in problem framing?
**Answer:**  
The target variable defines what the model should predict and guides the entire modeling process.

---

## Q10. What is a tensor in the context of data science and machine learning?
**Answer:**  
A tensor is a multi-dimensional array used to represent data in machine learning.

---

## Q11. How are tensors different from vectors and matrices?
**Answer:**  
- Scalar: 0D tensor  
- Vector: 1D tensor  
- Matrix: 2D tensor  
- Tensor: 3D or higher dimensions  

---

## Q12. Why are tensors important in deep learning frameworks like TensorFlow and PyTorch?
**Answer:**  
Tensors enable efficient computation on GPUs, handle large-scale data, and are the core data structure for neural networks.
