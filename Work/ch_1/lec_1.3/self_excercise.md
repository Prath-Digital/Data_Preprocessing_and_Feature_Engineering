# Self Exercise - 1.3

## Q1. What is Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), and why is it important in data science?
**Answer:**  
EDA is the process of analyzing and summarizing datasets using statistics and visualization techniques.  
It is important because it helps understand the data, detect patterns, identify anomalies, and prepare data for modeling.

---

## Q2. What are the main goals of performing EDA before building models?
**Answer:**  
- Understand data structure and types  
- Handle missing values  
- Detect outliers  
- Identify relationships between variables  
- Prepare clean data for modeling  

---

## Q3. How does EDA help in identifying patterns, anomalies, and relationships in data?
**Answer:**  
EDA uses visualization (graphs, plots) and statistics to reveal trends, unusual values, and correlations between variables.

---

## Q4. What is univariate analysis, and when is it used?
**Answer:**  
Univariate analysis examines a single variable to understand its distribution, central tendency, and spread.  
It is used during the initial stage of data analysis.

---

## Q5. Which statistical measures are commonly used in univariate analysis?
**Answer:**  
- Mean  
- Median  
- Mode  
- Variance  
- Standard deviation  

---

## Q6. What types of plots are typically used for univariate analysis?
**Answer:**  
- Histogram  
- Boxplot  
- Density plot  

---

## Q7. What is the difference between bivariate and multivariate analysis?
**Answer:**  
- Bivariate analysis studies the relationship between two variables  
- Multivariate analysis studies relationships among more than two variables  

---

## Q8. Which visualization methods are best suited for bivariate analysis?
**Answer:**  
- Scatter plots  
- Correlation heatmaps  
- Line plots  

---

## Q9. How does multivariate analysis help in understanding interactions between multiple variables?
**Answer:**  
It helps identify complex relationships and interactions between multiple variables, improving insights and model accuracy.

---

## Q10. Give an example of a real-world scenario where multivariate analysis is useful.
**Answer:**  
Predicting house prices using variables like location, size, number of rooms, and age of property.

---

## Q11. What is Pandas Profiling, and how does it simplify EDA?
**Answer:**  
Pandas Profiling is a Python library that generates automated EDA reports with statistics and visualizations.

---

## Q12. What types of information does a Pandas Profiling report provide automatically?
**Answer:**  
- Data summary  
- Missing values  
- Correlations  
- Distributions  
- Outliers  
- Data types
